Information-Theoretic Fixed Points in Inference-Time Mathematical Reasoning
A Technical Note on Aggregation, Control, and Verification for AIMO-Style Systems
Abstract

We present a compact information-theoretic framework for inference-time mathematical reasoning, motivated by practical architectures used in AIMO-style competitions. The framework decomposes reasoning into three layers: (i) a rigorous aggregation estimator for modular answers, (ii) a variational control law for adaptive proof search, and (iii) a fixed-point diagnostic for verifier stability. Together, these components formalize how correct mathematical solutions emerge as stable fixed points under noisy generation, bounded computation, and iterative verification.
